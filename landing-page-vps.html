Frequently Asked Questions

A VPS (Virtual Private Server) gives you dedicated resources — RAM, CPU, and storage — on a server that's partitioned just for you. "Managed" means we take care of the server-side work: security updates, patches, monitoring, and backups. You get the performance and control of a VPS without needing to be a Linux sysadmin. Think of it as having your own server with a built-in IT team.
With shared web hosting, your site shares server resources (CPU, RAM, bandwidth) with other customers. That's fine for smaller sites, but as you grow, you'll notice the limits. A VPS gives you guaranteed, dedicated resources — your own slice of the server. That means more consistent performance, better handling of traffic spikes, and the ability to customize your server environment. If your shared hosting feels sluggish or you're running multiple sites, VPS is the natural next step.
Not with a managed VPS. We handle the server management — updates, security, monitoring — so you can focus on your websites and applications. You'll use the same familiar control panel for day-to-day tasks like installing WordPress, managing domains, and setting up email. If you ever need to dig deeper, full SSH access is available. But most customers never need it.
That's where Auto-Boost RAM Protection comes in. If your site experiences a sudden surge in traffic, we automatically allocate additional RAM to keep things running smoothly. You'll get an alert so you know what happened, but your visitors won't notice a thing. There are no overage fees — it's included with every plan. If you're consistently hitting your resource limits, we'll recommend the right time to upgrade.
