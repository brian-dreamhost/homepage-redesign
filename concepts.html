Homepage Concepts

Full-page prototypes exploring different strategic directions for the DreamHost homepage.

Concept A
Commercial / Price-Led

Plan cards front and center. Hosting pricing leads the page at $2.95/mo, followed by Remixer as the easy alternative. Performance data and reliability benchmarks close the deal. The Hostinger/GoDaddy playbook — price-first, trust-second — adapted for DreamHost.

Unlike competitors: DreamHost shows renewal pricing upfront (Hostinger hides 5.5x increases), leads with 0% failure rate data (no competitor publishes load test results), and positions Remixer as an honest alternative rather than forcing everyone through the same funnel.

Concept B
Remixer-Led

AI website builder as the headline. $1.99/mo Netflix pricing anchor. "Built with Remixer" gallery. Hosting plans positioned as the engine underneath. Target: the no-dashboard crowd who just wants to be online.

Unlike competitors: GoDaddy wraps everything under "Airo" branding but still requires a dashboard. Hostinger's AI is bolt-on. Remixer IS the product — describe what you want, watch it build. No competitor offers a truly conversational site builder at this price point.
Coming soon
Concept C
Social Proof / Trust-Led

Trustpilot rating in the hero. Customer testimonials, "Built with DreamHost" showcase, vertical-specific examples. Let customers sell for you.

Unlike competitors: SiteGround puts Trustpilot 4.9 in the hero but stops there. BlueHost stacks WordPress.org badges. DreamHost can combine trust data with real performance benchmarks (0% failure rate) and 27 years of independence — a trust story no PE-owned competitor can tell.
Coming soon
Concept D
Performance-Led

Lead with the data no competitor can match. 0% failure rate under load, sub-20ms TTFB, 3 datacenter regions. ReviewSignals benchmarks, speed comparison charts. The "infrastructure nerds" angle.

Unlike competitors: BlueHost publishes one number (247ms). Nobody else publishes any. DreamHost has K6 load test data showing 0% failure while WPEngine hits 13.5% and Kinsta hits 93.4%. This is the only concept where the data IS the differentiator, not just supporting evidence.
Coming soon
Concept E
Audience-Led (Small Business)

We are your partner. Homepage organized around who you are and what you're building — not what we sell. Small business owners see themselves in customer stories, vertical-specific examples, and growth-focused messaging. Plans positioned as the engine behind your success.

Unlike competitors: Every competitor organizes by product (hosting, domains, email). This concept organizes by outcome — "I want to launch a restaurant site" or "I need an online store." DreamHost becomes the partner, not the vendor. SiteGround's Trustpilot-in-hero approach meets Hostinger's customer gallery, but with DreamHost's human support story as the backbone.
Coming soon
Concept F
GoDaddy-Inspired

Domain search IS the hero CTA. Tabbed product navigator below (Hosting, WordPress, Domains, Email, Remixer) lets visitors self-select. Airo-style unified AI branding applied to Remixer. Customer video testimonials and an extended FAQ section for SEO.

Unlike GoDaddy: DreamHost can run this playbook without the bloat. GoDaddy's page has 11 sections selling everything to everyone. This version keeps the domain-first capture and product tabs but cuts the noise — fewer sections, honest pricing, no "Airo every day" repetition.
Coming soon
Concept G
BlueHost-Inspired

Trust stacking from the first pixel. Hero leads with WordPress.org recommendation, 30-day guarantee, and "1.5 million websites" stat. Speed benchmarks section with real TTFB data. Phone number in the header. FAQ section at the bottom for SEO. Pricing buried after trust-building.

Unlike BlueHost: BlueHost publishes one speed number (247ms) with no methodology. DreamHost has K6 load test data, 0% failure rate, and 3 datacenter regions. Same trust-first structure, but backed by data BlueHost can't match — plus 27 years of independence vs. PE ownership.
Coming soon
Concept H
Hostinger-Inspired

Price-first conversion machine. Countdown timer in promo bar. Hero sells one number ($2.95/mo) with a "Claim Deal" CTA. Dense page: 12+ sections covering website builder, email, customer gallery, press logos, and a full pricing table at the bottom. Volume stats (1.5M sites, 27 years).

Unlike Hostinger: Hostinger's $1.99 requires a 48-month commitment that renews at 5.5x. DreamHost shows renewal pricing upfront and offers a 97-day money-back guarantee (vs. Hostinger's 30 days). Same urgency-driven format, but with honest pricing and no bait-and-switch.
Coming soon
Concept I
SiteGround-Inspired

The leanest page of all. No pricing on the homepage. Trustpilot 4.7 directly in the hero — let the rating do the selling. Six product cards with equal visual weight (Remixer, WordPress, Hosting, VPS, Email, Domains). "Real support, real humans 24/7" as an explicit counter to the AI-everything trend.

Unlike SiteGround: SiteGround aggregates everyone else's AI (ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini) into "AI Studio." DreamHost built Remixer — a first-party AI product, not a reseller bundle. Same premium-platform confidence, but with a proprietary AI story and 27 years of employee ownership as the trust backbone.
