DreamHost Homepage Redesign

What Hostinger, BlueHost, SiteGround, and GoDaddy put on their homepage

And what it means for DreamHost's redesign.

Executive Summary
All four competitors have converged on the same playbook — price or AI — and their homepage strategies reveal clear patterns in how each approaches conversion.
  • Pricing is a race to the bottom. Hostinger and BlueHost both advertise $1.99/mo. GoDaddy leads with $4.99/mo for domain + Airo AI. Hostinger requires a 48-month commitment that renews at 5.5x the intro rate. SiteGround shows no pricing at all.
  • AI is everywhere but owned by no one. GoDaddy has gone all-in on "Airo" as a branded AI product line. Hostinger has 13+ AI products, SiteGround aggregates ChatGPT/Claude/Gemini into AI Studio, BlueHost badges everything "AI built in." No competitor has made AI a defensible differentiator — though GoDaddy is betting the hardest with Airo branding across every section.
  • Trust strategies diverge sharply. Hostinger leads with press logos and volume stats (4M clients). BlueHost publishes speed benchmarks (247ms) and stacks WordPress.org credibility. SiteGround puts Trustpilot 4.9 directly in the hero. GoDaddy plays the scale card — "millions of customers" and "20+ million trust us" — leaning on sheer volume over quality signals.
  • Ownership goes unmentioned. BlueHost is PE-owned (Newfold Digital). Hostinger is VC-backed. SiteGround was acquired. GoDaddy is publicly traded (NYSE: GDDY). None of them talk about it — but the ownership structures shape every pricing, support, and product decision on these pages.

Competitor Portraits

Four homepages, four strategies

Hostinger
Price-first, AI-everywhere
"Launch your website today with 85% off"

Hostinger's homepage is a conversion machine built around a single number: $1.99. The hero doesn't mention AI, WordPress, or any specific product — it sells a price point and creates urgency with a countdown timer. Product education comes later. AI products (Horizons, Kodee, AI Website Builder) are positioned mid-page under the balanced headline "Human-led, AI-powered" — acknowledging AI skepticism while still claiming capability. The page is dense: 12 sections covering everything from email marketing to a customer website gallery. Pricing requires a 48-month commitment and renews at $10.99-$25.99/mo (a 5.5x increase over introductory pricing).

BlueHost
WordPress identity, trust stacking
"Grow Online with Confidence"

BlueHost's homepage stacks trust signals like a closing argument. The hero leads with credibility: WordPress.org recommendation, 30-day guarantee, "5 million WordPress users." There's a phone number in the top bar — unique among the three — signaling a consultation-first sales model. Every product, feature, and CTA ties back to WordPress. The page's most distinctive asset is a speed benchmark section showing BlueHost's 247ms US load time compared to competitors in a bar chart. Pricing is buried in section 8 of 10, after extensive trust-building. The page ends with a FAQ section (strong for SEO) and a "Request a consultation" lead gen form.

SiteGround
Premium platform, no price on the page
"Build, host, grow your online presence"

SiteGround's homepage is the leanest and most confident of the three. No pricing, no urgency, no discounts. The hero puts Trustpilot's 4.9/5 stars directly in the hero — letting the rating do the selling. Below, six product cards are shown with equal visual weight (WordPress, Website Builder, Online Store, Web Hosting, Email Marketing, Coderick AI) — a deliberate "platform" statement. The most distinctive move: AI Studio, included in all plans, which aggregates ChatGPT, Claude, Google, and Gemini into one dashboard. Instead of building proprietary AI, SiteGround gives you access to everyone else's. The support section — "Real support, real humans 24/7" with 98% satisfaction for 10+ years — is the sharpest counter-positioning against the AI-everything trend.

GoDaddy
Domain-first, AI brand play with Airo
"Get started for just $4.99"

GoDaddy's homepage is built around a single brand bet: Airo. The hero leads with a domain search bar bundled with "includes Airo™" — the AI product line that touches every section of the page. Below the hero, a tabbed product navigator ("Domains", "Websites", "Create a Logo", "Security & Hosting", "Email & Microsoft 365", "Names over tea") lets users self-select into product paths. The page then builds a case for GoDaddy AI across three major sections: AI-powered tools (logo, site builder, social), a "powerful website builder" showcase, and an "Airo every day, all the way" testimonial section with a customer video and feature checklist. The "Everyone works with GoDaddy" section plays the scale card — multiple audience types, "20+ million trust us for domains." The page closes with another video case study, a "We love to help. Seriously." support section with phone number and FAQ, and a dense bottom nav. The FAQ section is notably long (8+ questions) — a clear SEO play.
